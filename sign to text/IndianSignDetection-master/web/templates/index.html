About Indian Sign Language

Indian Sign Language (ISL) is a visual-gestural language used by the deaf and hard-of-hearing community in India. With its own grammar and syntax distinct from spoken languages, ISL serves as a primary means of communication for approximately 6.3 million deaf individuals across the country.


Unlike American Sign Language (ASL) which often uses single-hand gestures, ISL frequently involves both hands, making it uniquely complex and expressive. VISTA aims to bridge the communication gap by making ISL accessible to everyone through AI-powered real-time translation.
